### Day 1: Upper A

On your first day, the focus is on the upper body, specifically targeting the chest, back, shoulders, and arms. You'll start with the **Bench Press**, which is a fundamental exercise for building chest strength and muscle mass. Perform 4 sets of 6-8 reps, allowing 120 seconds of rest between sets. Make sure to lower the bar steadily and press up with control to engage your chest thoroughly.

Next is the **Lat Pulldown**, crucial for strengthening your back. Complete 4 sets of 8-10 reps, with a 90-second rest after each set. Focus on bringing the bar to your upper chest and squeezing your shoulder blades together to maximize back activation.

Lastly, you'll perform the **Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press** to target your shoulders. Execute 3 sets of 8-10 reps, resting for 90 seconds between sets. Keep your back straight and push the dumbbells overhead without locking your elbows to maintain shoulder tension.

This upper/lower split is ideal for beginners, as it provides a balanced approach to building overall strength and muscle. With four training days per week, it allows for sufficient rest and recovery, which is crucial for progress, especially at the start of your fitness journey.

### Practical Tips:
- **Rest Time**: Stick to the suggested rest times closely to maintain workout intensity.
- **Exercise Execution**: Use proper form to prevent injury. Start with lighter weights to master the technique before progressing.
- **Recovery**: Prioritize good nutrition, hydration, and sleep to aid muscle recovery and growth. This will improve your performance over time.
