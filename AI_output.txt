### Day 1: Upper A

This day focuses on the upper body, specifically targeting the chest, back, shoulders, and arms. Starting with the Bench Press, you'll perform 4 sets of 6-8 reps. This exercise is fundamental for developing chest strength and size. Make sure to control the weight both on the way down and up, and ensure you're using a full range of motion. Rest for 120 seconds between sets to allow for recovery and maintain strength.

Next, the Lat Pulldown will target your back, primarily the lats. With 4 sets of 8-10 reps, this exercise will help develop a V-shaped upper body. Focus on pulling down with your elbows rather than your hands to fully engage the back muscles. Take 90 seconds of rest between sets.

Finish with the Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press, performing 3 sets of 8-10 reps. This exercise will build your shoulder strength. Keep your back straight and avoid arching as you press the weight above your head. Again, take 90 seconds of rest between sets.

This upper-body day begins the week strong, setting a foundation for muscle building and strength development. The structured rest times are designed to ensure you maintain performance throughout each session.

For practical tips, ensure you warm up before starting the session with some dynamic stretches or light cardio. Focus on technique over weight to build a strong foundation. Post-workout, incorporate some light stretches to aid recovery and reduce muscle soreness. At a beginner level, listening to your body is crucial; if you're feeling overly fatigued, adjust accordingly.
