### Day 1: Upper A

On the first day of your workout plan, you're focusing on your upper body, specifically the chest, back, shoulders, and arms. The workout is a great way to build strength and muscle in these areas. You'll start with the Bench Press, which targets your chest, shoulders, and triceps. Aim for 4 sets of 6-8 reps with a rest of 120 seconds between sets. This exercise is key for building upper body strength.

Next, you'll move on to the Lat Pulldown. This exercise works your back and helps improve posture and back strength. Do 4 sets of 8-10 reps, resting for 90 seconds between each set. 

Finally, the Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press will target your shoulder muscles. You’ll do 3 sets of 8-10 reps with a 90-second rest between sets. Make sure to keep a controlled motion and avoid arching your back during this exercise.

### Day 2: Lower A

The second day focuses on your lower body, including quads, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. Starting with the Back Squat, you'll work on building strength in your quads and glutes. Perform 4 sets of 6-8 reps, resting for 120 seconds between each set. Ensure your knees are aligned with your toes.

Next, Romanian Deadlifts are designed to strengthen your hamstrings and glutes while also engaging your lower back. Do 4 sets of 8-10 reps with the same 120-second rest period.

Finish with Standing Calf Raises to target your calves. You'll do 4 sets of 12-15 reps, resting for 60 seconds. Focus on a slow and steady movement to get the most out of this exercise.

### Day 3: Upper B

Back to the upper body, Day 3 complements Day 1 by varying the movements and slightly altering the muscle focus. Begin with the Incline Dumbbell Press, which shifts some emphasis to the upper chest. Aim for 4 sets of 8-10 reps with 90 seconds of rest.

Pull Ups are next, a fantastic exercise for your back, biceps, and overall upper body strength. Perform 3 sets of 6-8 reps with 120-second rest gaps.

Complete the session with Hammer Curls to focus on your biceps and forearms. Go for 3 sets of 10-12 reps, focusing on a controlled movement and taking 60 seconds to rest in between sets.

### Day 4: Lower B

On the final day, you'll target your lower body once more but with different exercises to keep a balanced program. Start with the Deadlift to work on overall lower body strength, as well as core stability. Perform 3 sets of 5-6 reps, allowing for a longer rest of 150 seconds due to the intensity of this compound exercise.

The Bulgarian Split Squat focuses on each leg individually, great for balance and unilateral strength. Do 3 sets of 8-10 reps per leg, with 90 seconds rest between sets. 

Lastly, the Hip Thrust targets your glutes. Perform 3 sets of 8-10 reps with a 90-second rest in between. Focus on engaging your core and squeezing your glutes at the top for maximum benefit.
